Who Am I?

Exploring different paths in life.

Introduction

Who Am I? is an immersive VR interactive experience built in Unity, designed to investigate a philosophical question: The unreachable possibilities of life, and the regrets shaped not by what has happened, but by what never came to be.

The work does not ask what we possess, but rather what we never lived yet constantly imagine—those futures suspended outside the arc of our decisions, fading from reality while growing sharper in imagination. The project reaches toward the abandoned versions of the self, the shadow-selves who could have been, and the human tendency to romanticize and beautify the road left unchosen.

The Experience

Players enter a world of absolute white—unnamed, unformed, like memory before it takes root or a life before it takes shape. In the beginning, there is nothing; only through action does existence emerge. By throwing spheres of pigment into the void, players reveal hidden architecture, giving form and color to blank space. Each throw becomes an act of creation.

In every space, two doors await—each leading to a distinct environment and emotional trajectory. The experience begins with a simple yet universal question:

“At eighteen, do I stay where I was born, or do I go elsewhere to begin anew?”

But the path does not remain singular. It branches into four possible endings. The narrative does not unfold linearly, but grows from the player’s decisions—story is not told, but discovered through movement. Each space is accompanied by fragments of voice-over—imagery, sensation, intuition rather than exposition.

The work invites players to dwell in the tension between choosing and hesitating, to feel the weight of possibility and the quiet ache of what is absent.

Regret is rarely born from what we lost, but from what never happened at all.
