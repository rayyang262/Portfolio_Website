Projects

My design practice sits at the intersection of aesthetics and environmental responsibility.
I design products that change behavior — not by lecturing, but by making the
sustainable option more intuitive and more appealing.

This started early. Growing up with grandparents in architecture and art, I learned that design decisions carry real environmental consequences. That principle drives every project: from a portable speaker system engineered for spatial audio, to an eco-friendly battery disposal unit designed to reward recycling behavior and replace the generic bins people ignore.

I treat beauty as functional. If a sustainable product doesn’t attract use, it fails. My work combines material research, user behavior analysis, and visual design to close that gap.

EP. 01 — The Illuminated Sounds

Category: Product Design  •  Aug 2022 – Jan 2023

A portable speaker system engineered for spatial audio — a concept that pushes the boundaries of immersive sound design. Combines modular hardware design with adaptive volume regulation to deliver immersive sound without auditory risk.

EP. 02 — Footsteps of Impact

Category: Interactive Media Arts  •  Aug 2022 – Jan 2023

An interactive art installation connecting human movement to environmental consequence. Viewers step through a space as projected visuals degrade in real time, making the invisible impact of human activity viscerally felt.

EP. 03 — ProcrastiNoMore

Category: UX Design  •  Feb – April 2023

A behavior-change UX system that reframes productivity not as discipline but as design. Multi-surface displays (desk, ceiling, entrance) keep tasks visible in everyday environments, reducing avoidance and increasing follow-through.

EP. 04 — Battery Saver

Category: Product Design  •  Feb – May 2023

An eco-friendly battery disposal unit that makes responsible recycling feel satisfying rather than effortful. Makes the right choice the path of least resistance by redesigning the disposal experience from the ground up.

  • View Project

EP. 05 — The Tree of Duration

Category: Interactive Media Art  •  Oct – Dec 2025

A tree installation inspired by Henri Bergson’s philosophy of perception and time. Four screens within a fabricated trunk display the lifecycle of a tree; proximity-triggered audio and randomized visuals immerse viewers in the act of perceiving change.

  • View Project

EP. 06 — Who Am I?

Category: Unity Game  •  Oct – Dec 2025

An immersive VR experience in Unity exploring life’s unreachable possibilities. Players throw pigment into a white void to reveal architecture, then navigate branching doors toward four possible endings—each shaped by the weight of unchosen paths.
