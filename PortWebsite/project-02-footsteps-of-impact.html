Footsteps of Impact

An interactive art project connecting human movement to environmental consequence.

Introduction

Footsteps of Impact is an interactive art project that aims to inspire people to rethink the impact of human activities on the natural environment. Through interactive images and installations, the project invites viewers to move within a specific space and simulate the changes in the impact of human behavior on the natural environment over time through their footsteps. By providing viewers with a unique experience, the project aims to help them gain a deeper understanding of the effects of human activities on the natural ecology under the concept of human-centered thinking. This interactive approach enables viewers to fully comprehend the impact of human activities on the natural environment and can also increase their awareness of environmental protection, thereby better protecting our planet.

Technical Architecture

The installation uses an FSR force sensor to detect audience interaction through pressure or stepping. An ESP32 microcontroller processes this input and sends data to TouchDesigner, where real-time visuals are generated. The system also controls a DC TT motor through an L9110S driver to move physical elements in response to interaction. A projector outputs the visuals, linking body movement, mechanical response, and projected media into one interactive loop.

The system is organized around five primary components. A ceiling-mounted projector casts visuals toward a large frontal screen that serves as the main display surface. Suspended from an overhead lifting track are movable objects or panels that can be raised, lowered, or repositioned to alter the projected composition and spatial atmosphere. These hanging elements function both as sculptural components and as dynamic surfaces that can interrupt, catch, or reshape the projection.

Image Interaction (TouchDesigner)

Stage Visual State Meaning
0 steps Clear, vivid greens The unpolluted Earth before human activity.
1st step Screen distorts, turns grey and blurry How a single person begins to damage the planet.
2nd step Trash bags dropping, background shaking Accumulating impact of continued behavior.
3rd step Black & white, dystopian A degraded, desolate environment.
Final stage Completely distorted, only trash remains The worst-case environmental scenario.

At first, the screen shows a clear background with no sign of pollution—a lot of green, representing the unpolluted Earth. With each step, the environment progressively degrades until the background is completely distorted black and white, with only the trash remaining in the foreground.
